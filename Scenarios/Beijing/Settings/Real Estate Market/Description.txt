MaxCandidate: The maximum number of candidate facilities that can be selected by businessmen.
MaxNegotiation: The maximum number of negotiations that can take place between businessmen and property owners.
StopNegotiation: If the price change during negotiations is lower than this threshold, negotiations will stop.
Loss: A parameter regarding the loss effect, primarily used in calculating the capacity of residential facilities, but can be applied to commercial facilities as well.
FlePur: A parameter determining how many flexible triggers a businessman needs to decide whether to move into a purchased commercial facility.
FleRent: A parameter determining how many flexible triggers a businessman needs to decide whether to move into a rented commercial facility.
ResPriRisDeg: The rate at which the price of a commercial facility increases, affecting the rental cost for businessmen.
ResPriDesDeg: The rate at which the price of a commercial facility decreases, affecting the rental cost for businessmen.
ResPriRisAtt: A threshold that defines when the attractiveness of a commercial facility exceeds it, causing the price to rise, thus affecting the decisions of businessmen.
ResPriDesAtt: A threshold that defines when the attractiveness of a commercial facility falls below it, causing the price to decrease, impacting businessman leasing decisions.
ResPurAcc: The weight of accessibility when calculating the utility of a commercial property for purchase by businessmen.
ResPurPrice: The weight of the price when calculating the utility of a commercial property for purchase by businessmen.
PurAfford: A threshold determining whether a businessman can afford to purchase a commercial property.
ResPurProspect: A parameter representing the expected utility for a businessman; they will only purchase a commercial facility if the utility exceeds this threshold.
ResRenRisDeg: The rate at which the rent of a commercial facility increases, influencing the decision of businessmen to lease.
ResRenDesDeg: The rate at which the rent of a commercial facility decreases, influencing the decision of businessmen to lease.
ResRenRisAtt: A threshold that defines when the attractiveness of a commercial facility exceeds it, causing the rent to rise, affecting businessman leasing decisions.
ResRenDesAtt: A threshold that defines when the attractiveness of a commercial facility falls below it, causing the rent to decrease, influencing businessman leasing decisions.
ResRentAcc: The weight of accessibility when calculating the utility of a commercial facility for leasing.
ResRentPrice: The weight of rent price when calculating the utility of a commercial facility for leasing.
RentAfford: A threshold determining whether a businessman can afford the rent for a commercial facility.
ResRentProspect: A parameter representing the expected utility; businessmen will only rent a commercial facility if the utility exceeds this threshold.
FMoRent: The weight of rent in the utility calculation for a businessman’s decision to move into a commercial facility.
FMoAcc: The weight of accessibility in the utility calculation for a businessman’s decision to move into a commercial facility.
FMoAgg: The weight of agglomeration in the utility calculation for a businessman’s decision to move into a commercial facility.
FMoSpace: The weight of space in the utility calculation for a businessman’s decision to move into a commercial facility.
FMoThre: A threshold; if the move utility for a businessman exceeds this value, they will decide to relocate their business to a new facility.
OffPrice: The weight of the price when calculating the utility of an office facility for a businessman.
OffAcc: The weight of accessibility when calculating the utility of an office facility for a businessman.
OffAgg: The weight of agglomeration when calculating the utility of an office facility for a businessman.
OffRentProspect: A parameter representing the expected utility; businessmen will only lease an office facility if the utility exceeds this threshold.
OffRisDeg: The rate at which the price of an office facility increases, affecting the cost for businessmen.
OffDesDeg: The rate at which the price of an office facility decreases, influencing businessman decisions to lease or stay.

OffDesAtt: A threshold that defines when the attractiveness of an office facility falls below it, causing rent to decrease.
OffRentUpper: The upper limit on the rent increase for office facilities within the current simulation year.
OffRentLower: The lower limit on the rent decrease for office facilities within the current simulation year.
